After the Great Flood: The Project Glossary

By: Kyeesha M. Wilcox, April 30, 2021

A

Adobe Illustrator
A graphic design application from Adobe. The tool is used for creating and editing images for maps, illustrations, and other digital and print graphic design projects.

ArcGIS/ArcMap
A software for working with geographic data and creating maps. The geographic information system (GIS) can be used for creating shapefiles, geocoding coordinates and using existing geographic data to plot information into various styles of maps.

American Community Survey
An annual survey administered throughout American households by the U.S. Census Bureau. The American Community Survey (ACS) collects data on four characteristics: social, economic, housing and demographics. The ACS is offered at 1-, 3-, 5- and 10 year periods. The 5-year periods are considered the most reliable to use.

B

Bachelor's Degree of Higher
Percent of the total population 25 years and older that held at least a bachelor’s degree in a given census tract. One of the nine socioeconomic variables in the Modified Darden-Kamel Composite Socioeconomic Index. This variable is derived from the American Community Survey (ACS) and is calculated by adding the percentages for education categories beyond a bachelor’s degree in a given census tract (equation: bachelor’s degree% + master’s degree% + professional school degree% + doctorate degree%). The ACS variable name in Social Explorer is ‘Educational Attainment for Population 25 Years and Over’.

C

CSS
also known as Cascading Style Sheets. Used in conjunction with HTML to design and change presentation characteristics on documents and web pages.

Cultural Heritage Informatics Initiative
A fellowship program sponsored by Michigan State University’s Department of Anthropology. CHI hosts approximately 12-16 graduate students every year to explore digital methods and create an innovative project related to cultural heritage.

Cumberland River
A river stretching nearly 700 miles through Kentucky, Ohio and Tennessee.

D

Department of Geography, Environment and Spatial Sciences
An academic department at Michigan State University. The department offers undergraduate and graduate programs related to Geospatial Technologies, Nature-Society Studies, Physical Geography, Urban-Economic Geography and Medical/Health Geography.

Flourish
A visualization software that allows data to be inputted manually or imported and transformed into interactive graphs, charts and maps.

G

GitHub
A software development tool that uses Git to track and store various versions of coding projects. As of March 2021, GitHub’s free service for basic accounts offers users an unlimited amount of public and private repositories, unlimited amount of collaborators, 2,000 Actions minutes per month, 500MB of GitHub Packages storage and community support.

Great Flood of 2010

H

Hispanic
One of the categories of ethnicity defined by the U.S. Office of Management and Budget’s (OMB) definition. The U.S. Census Bureau further defined Hispanics or Latino as referring to “a person of Cuban, Mexican, Puerto Rican, South or Central American, or other Spanish culture or origin regardless or race”. Read the full definition on Census.gov.

Home ownership
Percent of the total population having owner-occupied housing units with a mortgage in a given census tract. One of the nine socioeconomic variables in the Modified Darden-Kamel Composite Socioeconomic Index. This variable is derived from the American Community Survey (ACS). The ACS variable name in Social Explorer is ‘Mortgage Status’ and the percent is obtained from the subcategories: ‘Owner-Occupied Housing Units’ > ‘Housing Units with a Mortgage, Home Equity Loan, or Similar Debts’.

HTML
also known as Hypertext Markup Language. Used to display text on webpages

M

Median household income
US Dollars of the median income of all family members 16 years and older, including those without income, in a given census tract. One of the nine socioeconomic variables in the Modified Darden-Kamel Composite Socioeconomic Index. This variable is derived from the American Community Survey (ACS). The ACS variable name in Social Explorer is ‘Median Household Income (In * Inflation Adjusted Dollars).

*For five year ACS results, is the last year in the year range. For instance, for the ACS in 2005-2009, the variable title would be ‘Median Household Income (In 2009 Inflation Adjusted Dollars).


Median house value
US Dollars of the median value of owner-occupied housing which is the respondent’s estimate of how much the property would sell for if it were for sale. One of the nine socioeconomic variables in the Modified Darden-Kamel Composite Socioeconomic Index. This variable is derived from the American Community Survey (ACS). The ACS variable name in Social Explorer is ‘Median House Value for All Owner-Occupied Housing Units’.

Median monthly rent
US Dollars of the contract rent value plus the estimated average monthly cost of utilities. One of the nine socioeconomic variables in the Modified Darden-Kamel Composite Socioeconomic Index. This variable is derived from the American Community Survey (ACS). The ACS variable name in Social Explorer is ‘Median Gross Rent’.

Metropolitan Nashville Area
Comprised of Davidson, Rutherford and Williamson counties. Some sources include more than three counties in their description of the Metropolitan Nashville Area. In order to be used with the project’s methodology, The Modified Darden-Kamel Composite Socioeconomic Index, only two to three counties could be included. For the purposes of this project, Davidson, Rutherford and Williamson were identified as the top three most populous counties in the Metropolitan area.

Modified Darden-Kamel Composite Socioeconomic Index
A methodology created by Dr. Joe T. Darden and Sameh M. Kamel. The methodology is used to determine the socioeconomic positions of census tracts in a two or three county area. Primarily three county areas have only been investigated. Researchers have used the methodology to identify trends in health outcomes, socioeconomic differences and residential segregation in Metropolitan Detroit. The methodology has not been used to investigate the Metropolitan Nashville area prior to this project.

The formula is as followed:

Where CSIi is the composite socioeconomic z-score index for census tract i, the sum of z scores for the socioeconomic status (SES) variables j, relative to the two-three county/metropolitan area; k is the number of variables in the index; Vij is the jth socioeconomic position (SEP) variable for a given census tract I; VjDMA is mean of the jth variable in the twothree country/metropolitan area; and S(VjDMA) is standard deviation of the jth variable in the two-three county/metropolitan area (Darden et al. 2010).


N

Nashville Great Flood of 2010
A natural disaster event in May 2010 that affected communities in Tennessee, Kentucky and Mississippi. In a short period of time, rain fell in great numbers (more than 19 inches in a two day period) resulting in several lives lost and many properties damaged.

Neighborhood Effects
Coined first in 1987 by Dr. William J. Wilson. Chances for ‘success’, whether that be education or job opportunities or mental and physical health, are positively or negatively affected by a neighborhood’s socioeconomic characteristics such high rates of unemployment, poverty, cultural heterogeneity, population turnover, changes in the job market and urban renewal.

Neighborhood Socioeconomic Characteristics
Are descriptors that represent a neighborhoods social and economic position in a community. These descriptors can include education levels, job status, race/ethnicity, poverty rates, internet access, transportation access, vehicle ownership, homeownership, income, crime rates and food access.

Not Hispanic or Latino
Refers to an individual that did not belong to either “...Cuban, Mexican, Puerto Rican, South or Central American, or [any] other Spanish culture or origin regardless or race”. For the purposes of this project, the shorter term ‘Non-Hispanic’ is interchangeable in meaning and has been used in place ‘Not Hispanic or Latino’ and conjunction with the races of ‘Black’ and ‘White’. See the definition for Hispanic or Latino.

Non-Hispanic Black
Refers to an individual that was not Hispanic or Latino and belonged to “any of the Black racial groups of Africa. It includes people who indicate their race as “Black or African Am.” or report entries such as African American, Kenyan, Nigerian, or Haitian” (109). For definition’s source, visit the U.S. Census Bureau’s 2015 Subject Definitions document.

Non-Hispanic White
Refers to an individual that was not Hispanic or Latino and belonged to racial groups of “...Europe, the Middle East, or North Africa. It includes people who indicate their race as “White” or report entries such as Irish, German, Italian, Lebanese, Arab, Moroccan, or Caucasian” (109). For definition’s source, visit the U.S. Census Bureau’s 2015 Subject Definitions document.

P

Poverty rate
Percent of the total population 18 to 64 years old that were living below the poverty line in the last year of the five year period in a given census tract. One of the nine socioeconomic variables in the Modified Darden-Kamel Composite Socioeconomic Index. This variable is derived from the American Community Survey (ACS), and its variable name in Social Explorer was ‘Poverty Status in * for Population Age 18 to 64’. The value was calculated by using the subcategory >Living in Poverty.

*For five year ACS results, is the last year in the year range. For instance, for the ACS in 2005-2009, the variable title would be ‘Poverty Status in 2009 for Population Age 18 to 64’.


Professional or managerial jobs
Percent of the total employed civilian population 16 years and over that had an occupation of either ‘professional and related occupations’ or ‘management, business, and financial operations occupations’. This variable is one of the nine socioeconomic variables in the Modified Darden-Kamel Composite Socioeconomic Index. This variable is derived from the American Community Survey (ACS), and its variable name in Social Explorer was ‘Occupation for Employed Civilian Population 16 Years and Over’. The value was calculated by adding the percentages of the two occupation categories in a given census tract (equation: professional and related occupations% + management, business, and financial operations occupations%).

S

SEP
Also known as socioeconomic position, which ranges from 1-5. As defined by the Modified Darden-Kamel Composite Socioeconomic Index, a neighborhood that receives an SEP of 1 experiences very low neighborhood socioeconomic characteristics; SEP 2 represents low neighborhood socioeconomic characteristics; SEP 3 represents middle neighborhood socioeconomic characteristics; SEP 4 represents high neighborhood socioeconomic characteristics; and SEP 5 represents very high neighborhood socioeconomic characteristics.

Social Explorer
A research tool that offers easy-to-access/view and downloadable tables of data, including the U.S. Census Bureau’s American Community Survey, at various geographical scales (i.e. nation, state, county, census tract). The tool also allows users to create thematic maps or view prepared maps created by Social Explorer on various topics.

T

ThingLink
A teaching tool that uses augmented reality technology for images, videos and virtual tours, according to their website. Users can add text, audio, videos to their images and 360-videos to create unique learning experiences for their viewers.

U

Unemployment rate
Percent of the total civilian labor force population that were 16 years and older that were unemployed in a given census tract. This variable is one of the nine socioeconomic variables in the Modified Darden-Kamel Composite Socioeconomic Index. The variable was derived from the American Community Survey (ACS), and its variable name was ‘Unemployment Rate for Civilian Population in Labor Force 16 Years and Over’. The value was found by using the subcategory >Unemployed.

V

Vehicle ownership
Percent of the population that had at least one vehicle as a means of transportation to work in a given census tract. This neighborhood socioeconomic characteristic is one of the nine socioeconomic variables in the Modified Darden-Kamel Composite Socioeconomic Index. The variable was derived from the American Community Survey (ACS), and its variable name was ‘Means of Transportation to Work by Vehicles Available’. The value was calculated by adding the percentages of the ‘total’ subcategory in a given census tract (equation: 1 vehicle available% + 2 vehicles available% + 3 or more vehicles available%). Alternatively, the same value could have been calculated by subtracting the percentage in >total >no vehicle available from 100%. The other subcategories and their corresponding figures were not included in this variable’s final values (i.e. ‘car, truck, or van - drove alone’, ‘car, truck, or van - carpooled’, ‘public transportation (exclusind taxicab)’, ‘walked’, ‘taxicab, motorcycle, bicycle, or other means’ and ‘worked at home’).

W

W3Schools
A site for those interested in web development and offers information on HTML, CSS, JavaScript, PHP, SQL, Python, Java, C++, C#, R, Bootstrap, jQuery, React, XML, W3.CSS, AI, Web Building, Sass, Angular and Git. The site has tutorials, exercises, examples, templates and code snippets for web developers in various stages of their learning.

Windshield survey
A survey conducted in Nashville and Davidson County. Damage assessment data was collected from residents on the damage to their property or properties. There were four levels of damage to choose from: either minor (1), moderate (2), major (3) or severe to destroyed (4). The data was visualized in an interactive map by Nashville's GIS Department in 2010.

About the Author

Kyeesha M. Wilcox (wilcox49@msu.edu) is a 2020 graduate of the master’s program in the Department of Geography, Environment and Spatial Sciences at Michigan State University.

After the Great Flood asked if the Metropolitan Nashville Area recovered socioeconomically from the flood, about the rate of gentrification in surrounding communities and how racial/ethnic neighborhood compositions changed after the flood. In this interactive project, viewers can explore photos of the damage, listen to interviews from survivors, learn about the geography of Nashville and see what socioeconomic and racial/ethnic changes occurred in neighborhoods after Nashville’s 2010 flood.
